Water ★★★

Water Sources & Cycle

  • Natural sources: River, Lake, Pond, Sea, Rain
  • Underground: Well, Borehole, Spring
  • Water Cycle: Evaporation → Condensation → Precipitation
  • Earth surface: 71% water — mostly saline (97.5%)
  • Fresh water only 2.5% — mostly glaciers e stored
💡1 person er per day minimum 50 litre water dorkar — WHO guideline

Water Conservation

  • Rainwater harvesting — cholapar jol dhorhe rakha
  • Drip irrigation — khete water save kora
  • Afforestation — gaach lagale beshi jol thake
  • Wastage rokha — leak fix kora
  • Jal Jeevan Mission — India Government scheme
💡World Water Day — 22 March. World Environment Day — 5 June.

EVS Pedagogy ★★★

Exam e guaranteed 8–10 questions from pedagogy

EVS Nature & Scope

  • EVS = Social Science + Science er integrated subject
  • Class I–II te Environment Studies (separate subject noy)
  • Class III–V te EVS as separate subject
  • Holistic understanding of environment
  • Child er immediate environment theke shuru
  • NCERT "Looking Around" series — Class 3, 4, 5

Teaching Methods

  • Field Trip — direct observation, real environment
  • Project Method — real problem solve kora
  • Story Telling — concept ke narrative e dhoranো
  • Role Play — shishu nijer role play kore shikhey
  • Discussion — open-ended questions e thought build
  • Experimentation — simple experiments diye concept

Good EVS Questions — Characteristics

  • Open-ended howa — ek answer nei
  • Child er experience relate kora
  • Critical thinking develop kora
  • Curiosity o exploration encourage kora
  • Environment er direct observation involve kora
💡EVS e rote memorization discourage kora hoy — understanding o application e focus

Assessment in EVS

  • Portfolio — shishu er collected work
  • Observation — classroom behavior note kora
  • Oral questions — discussion e participation
  • Drawing & Activity — creative expression
  • Field visit report — real experience document
  • Group project — collaborative work
