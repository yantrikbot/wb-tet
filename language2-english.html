Tenses — All 12 Forms ★★★

Present Tenses

Simple Present
V1 / V1+s/es
She reads every day.
Present Continuous
am/is/are + V-ing
She is reading now.
Present Perfect
has/have + V3
She has read the book.
Present Perfect Continuous
has/have been + V-ing
She has been reading since morning.

Past Tenses

Simple Past
V2 (ed form)
She read yesterday.
Past Continuous
was/were + V-ing
She was reading when I called.
Past Perfect
had + V3
She had read before I came.
Past Perfect Continuous
had been + V-ing
She had been reading for 2 hours.

Future Tenses

Simple Future
will/shall + V1
She will read tomorrow.
Future Continuous
will be + V-ing
She will be reading at 5 PM.
Future Perfect
will have + V3
She will have read by 6 PM.
Future Perfect Continuous
will have been + V-ing
She will have been reading for 2 hrs.

Voice & Narration ★★★

Active → Passive Voice

TenseActivePassive
Simple PresentHe writes a letter.A letter is written by him.
Simple PastHe wrote a letter.A letter was written by him.
Simple FutureHe will write.It will be written by him.
Present PerfectHe has written.It has been written by him.
💡Object → Subject hoy. Subject "by" phrase e jায়। Verb: be + V3

Direct → Indirect Narration

  • Reporting verb: said → said / told (person ache hole "told")
  • Inverted commas: remove kore conjunction "that" add
  • Pronoun change: I → he/she, we → they, you → he/she
  • Tense backshift: Present → Past, Past → Past Perfect
  • Time words: now→then, today→that day, tomorrow→next day
💡Question e: if/whether add kora hoy. Command e: told + to + V1

Articles & Prepositions ★★

Articles — a, an, the

aConsonant sound er age: a book, a university (u=yoo sound)
anVowel sound er age: an apple, an hour (h silent)
theSpecific, unique, superlative, instrument, river/mountain name er age
No articleProper nouns, languages, sports, meals (have breakfast)

Common Prepositions

atSpecific time/place: at 5 PM, at school
inMonths/years/cities: in January, in Delhi
onDays/dates: on Monday, on 5th June
sincePoint of time: since 2010, since morning
forDuration: for 2 hours, for many years
byDeadline: by 5 PM. Agent: done by him

Error Correction in Classroom

  • Recast — Shishu er bhul correct form e repeat kora
  • Clarification Request — "Tumi ki boltey chaicho?" — shishu nijer fix kore
  • Elicitation — Incomplete sentence diye shishu complete korate deoa
  • Metalinguistic clue — "Is that correct grammar?" hint deoa
  • Explicit correction — "Wrong, it should be..."
💡Fluency er time e error over-correct kora ucit noy — confidence toita hoy

Reading Skills

  • Extensive reading — Pleasure er jonno, much reading
  • Inferencing — Text theke implicit meaning ber kora

Writing Skills Development
