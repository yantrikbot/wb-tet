Bridho o Bikash — Growth & Development ★★★

Growth vs Development · Stages · Principles
📏

Growth vs Development

  • Growth — quantitative change: height, weight (measurable)
  • Development — qualitative change: thinking, emotion, skill
  • Growth stops (adulthood), Development lifelong
  • Growth = part of development
💡Trick: Growth = ruler diye mapte paro · Development = mapte para jay na
📐

Bikasher Niti — Principles

  • Continuous & gradual process
  • Cephalocaudal — head to toe
  • Proximodistal — centre to periphery
  • Individual differences exist
  • Heredity + Environment duto miliye
  • Interrelated (physical, mental, social)

Bikasher Bivinno Stor (Stages)

StageAgeKey Feature
Infancy0–2 yrsRapid physical growth, motor development, sensory learning
Early Childhood2–6 yrsLanguage, play, imagination, self-concept begins
Late Childhood ★6–12 yrsSchool age — cognitive, social, moral development (TET focus)
Adolescence12–18 yrsPuberty, identity, abstract thinking, peer influence

Heredity & Environment ★★

Nature vs Nurture — Bikashe provab
🧬

Heredity (Vorsho)

  • Parents theke genes-er madhome provab
  • IQ, personality, physical traits transmit hoy
  • Galton — first studied heredity and intelligence
  • Twin studies — identical twins same traits show kore
🌍

Environment (Poribesh)

  • Family, school, peers, culture — sob influence kore
  • Watson (Behaviourism) — environment determines behaviour
  • Enriched environment → better cognitive development
  • Socio-economic status has major impact
Current View: Nature AND nurture — duto miliye bikash hoy. Neither alone is sufficient. Interaction of genes + environment = development.

Memory & Forgetting ★★

Smriti o Bismoron — Ebbinghaus
💾

Types of Memory

  • Sensory Memory — milliseconds, raw sensory input
  • Short-Term (Working) — 7±2 items, 20-30 seconds
  • Long-Term — unlimited, permanent store
  • Episodic — personal events memory
  • Semantic — general knowledge memory
  • Procedural — skills (cycle chalaano)
📉

Forgetting — Ebbinghaus Curve

  • After 1 hour — 50% forgotten
  • After 1 day — 70% forgotten
  • After 1 week — 90% forgotten (without review)
  • Spaced Repetition — best way to retain
  • Causes: Decay, Interference, Motivated forgetting
  • Retrieval failure — info ache, access nai
💡Review within 24 hours → retention doubles

Constructivism — Rachonashilotabad ★★★

Piaget + Vygotsky + Bruner — knowledge construction
🏗️

Core Idea

  • Shishu passive receiver noy — active knowledge builder
  • Prior knowledge + new experience = new understanding
  • Learning = meaning construction, not transmission
  • Cognitive Constructivism — Piaget (individual construction)
  • Social Constructivism — Vygotsky (social interaction e construction)
🔎

Bruner — Discovery Learning

  • Shishu nijei discover korbe — teacher guide korbe
  • Spiral Curriculum — same topic repeatedly, deeper level e
  • 3 modes: Enactive (do), Iconic (image), Symbolic (language)
  • Intrinsic motivation important
  • Narrative thinking — story diye shekhaona
💡EIS — Enactive → Iconic → Symbolic (age e baro)
Classroom Application: Project work, hands-on activities, group discussion, problem-based learning, inquiry-based teaching — all constructivist approaches

Remedial Teaching & Error Analysis ★★

Protikar Shikkhon · Bhul Bishleshon
🔧

Remedial Teaching

  • Learning difficulties diagnose korar pore specific help
  • Individual attention — small group ba 1-on-1
  • Simplified materials, visual aids, concrete objects
  • Step-by-step approach, frequent revision
  • Positive reinforcement crucial
  • Parent-teacher coordination
🔍

Error Analysis

  • Shishu r bhul = learning opportunity, punishment noy
  • Types of errors: Conceptual, Procedural, Careless
  • Systematic errors — pattern identify kora
  • Diagnostic testing → error pattern find → targeted remediation
  • Constructive feedback — "keno vul?" bujhiye dao
💡Error Analysis → Diagnose → Remediate → Reassess (cycle)
